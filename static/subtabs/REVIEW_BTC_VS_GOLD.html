Bitcoin vs Gold (troy ounce)

About Bitcoin vs Gold

This chart measures Bitcoin’s long-term valuation relative to physical gold, priced in troy ounces per BTC.

By denominating Bitcoin in gold instead of fiat currency, the dashboard removes distortions caused by monetary inflation, currency debasement, and foreign exchange volatility. This allows Bitcoin’s purchasing power to be evaluated against one of humanity’s oldest and most widely recognized stores of value.

Gold has historically functioned as hard money — scarce, durable, and resistant to inflationary supply expansion. Comparing Bitcoin to gold therefore provides insight into Bitcoin’s performance as a digital monetary asset rather than a speculative fiat-priced instrument.

When Bitcoin rises in gold terms, it indicates that BTC is appreciating faster than gold and gaining real purchasing power. When it falls, gold is outperforming Bitcoin as a store of value.

Users can explore Bitcoin’s valuation in gold across full history, individual years, halving cycles, or custom time ranges.

The logarithmic default scale reflects Bitcoin’s exponential monetization phases relative to gold over time.
