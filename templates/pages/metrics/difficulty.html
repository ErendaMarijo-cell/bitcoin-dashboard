About Bitcoin Difficulty

Bitcoin difficulty measures how hard it is to find a valid block compared to the easiest possible difficulty defined by the protocol.

It is recalculated every 2,016 blocks (≈ every 14 days) to maintain an average block interval of 10 minutes.

Rising difficulty reflects increasing network hash power, while declining difficulty may indicate miner capitulation or reduced mining participation.
