Bitcoin vs Silver (troy ounce)

About Bitcoin vs Silver

This chart measures Bitcoin’s long-term valuation relative to physical silver, priced in troy ounces per BTC. One troy ounce equals 31.1035 grams, allowing Bitcoin’s silver-denominated value to be evaluated in both ounce and gram terms — standard measurement units across global silver markets.

By denominating Bitcoin in silver rather than fiat currency, the chart provides insight into Bitcoin’s purchasing power relative to one of the world’s most widely traded precious metals.

Silver has historically functioned as both monetary metal and industrial commodity, bridging store-of-value demand with manufacturing and technology use cases. Comparing Bitcoin to silver therefore highlights Bitcoin’s performance not only as a digital store of value, but also relative to broader commodity and industrial markets.

Users can analyze the Bitcoin price in silver across full history, tracking how the BTC-to-silver ratio has evolved through market cycles, halving eras, and macroeconomic shifts. The chart illustrates how much silver one Bitcoin is worth over time.

When Bitcoin rises in silver terms, BTC is gaining purchasing power relative to silver. When it falls, silver is outperforming Bitcoin as a precious metal benchmark.

Time range filters allow analysis across full history, individual years, halving cycles, or fully custom date ranges.
